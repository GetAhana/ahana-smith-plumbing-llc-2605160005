Water heaters

Water heater service and replacement in Phoenix

A failing water heater is a stressful situation, especially in Arizona heat when a cold shower is the last thing any family needs. Smith Plumbing sizes the right unit—tank or tankless—for your household, handles the installation safely, and leaves you with clear warranty documentation and maintenance guidance so your hot water runs reliably for years to come.

Water heater sizing and installation specs

  • Tank sizing matched to your household: 40-gallon standard for families of 1–4, 50-gallon for 5+, or continuous-flow tankless for unlimited hot water and energy savings.
  • Gas or electric installation with proper venting—direct vent through roof for tank models, power venting for tankless, all sealed and code-compliant per Arizona regulations.
  • Pressure relief valve, expansion tank (for tankless), and all unions and fittings pressure-tested before we leave your home to ensure safe operation and prevent future leaks.
  • Old unit hauled away and disposed of properly, replaced model delivered and removed from your garage or utility room so the installation is clean and complete.

How we handle Water heaters

Water heater service follows a clear path from diagnosis to installation. We keep you informed at every stage so there are no surprises.

  • You contact us about your water heater — Tell us if the unit is leaking, making noise, producing rusty water, or simply failed. We ask about your household size, fuel type, and any special needs so we can recommend the right replacement.
  • We inspect and quote tank or tankless — Our technician checks your existing unit, current setup, and venting. We quote both tank and tankless options with upfront pricing, efficiency ratings, and warranty details so you understand the trade-offs.
  • We install, test, and document — Once approved, we haul away the old unit, install the new one safely, pressure-test all connections, and walk you through the warranty, maintenance schedule, and how to operate your new heater.
When to call us

Warning signs that your water heater needs service or replacement include rusty-colored water, strange noises from the tank, leaking around the base, or running out of hot water faster than usual. If you experience any of these in your Phoenix home, Smith Plumbing can diagnose the issue and recommend repair or replacement.

  • Rusty, brown, or metallic-tasting water often indicates an aging tank with internal corrosion—time to replace before a leak develops and damages your home.
  • Rumbling, popping, or hissing sounds from the tank signal sediment buildup or a developing fault—get a technician to assess whether flushing or replacement is the better option.
  • Visible leaking from the tank base or around fittings means failure is imminent—we can install a replacement the same day to avoid water damage and restore your hot water.
  • Your family runs out of hot water earlier than it used to, or showers are lukewarm even at full throttle—a sign the heater is losing efficiency and a replacement may save you money on utility bills.
  • Age is a factor: tank models typically last 8–12 years, tankless units 15–20 years. If yours is approaching or past that window, plan a replacement before emergency failure leaves you without hot water.

FAQ — Water heaters

What's the difference between a tank and tankless water heater?

Tank heaters store and continuously heat water in a 40–50 gallon tank, costing less upfront but taking up space and using energy even when no one is home. Tankless units heat water on demand, save energy and space, but cost more initially and may need venting upgrades. We help you choose based on your household size and budget.

How long does a water heater installation take?

Most replacements take three to five hours. We remove the old unit, install and vent the new one, pressure-test all connections, and haul away the old tank. We schedule enough time so you're not rushed and we can answer your questions before we leave.

Do you install both gas and electric water heaters?

Yes. We install gas, electric, and hybrid models, and we handle proper venting for gas units to meet Arizona code. If your current setup is electric and you want to switch to gas, we assess your existing piping and venting to see if upgrades are needed.
