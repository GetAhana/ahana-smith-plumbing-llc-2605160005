Slow drains and backups are never convenient, especially when they affect your kitchen, bathroom, or main sewer line. Drain clearing methods and documentation

  • Cable cleaning for localized kitchen or bathroom drain stoppages—motorized snake that breaks through grease, hair, and mineral buildup without harming your pipes.
  • Hydro-jetting for stubborn main-line clogs or heavily buildup sections—high-pressure water stream clears blockages and scours pipe walls clean, restoring full flow.
  • Sewer camera inspection with video documentation—HD scope navigates your drain line and identifies root intrusion, offset joints, bellies, or calcification so you understand the full condition.
  • Written findings report with photos showing the blockage location and method used to clear it, plus recommendations for prevention or future maintenance so you avoid costly repeats.

When to call us

Common signs of drain problems include slow water draining from sinks or showers, gurgling sounds from pipes, sewage odors inside or outside your home, or visible water pooling in your yard. If you notice any of these in your Phoenix home, call Smith Plumbing for a free drain diagnosis.

  • Slow drain flow in kitchen or bathroom is usually hair, grease, or mineral buildup—often cleared the same day with cable or hydro-jetting without needing expensive pipe replacement.
  • Gurgling sounds from pipes when you drain water, flush a toilet, or run a washing machine indicate a partial blockage or trapped air—a sign to call before the line backs up completely.
  • Raw sewage smell inside the home near drains or outside near the main cleanout is a serious warning sign—call immediately for camera inspection and clearing to prevent backups and health hazards.
  • Water pooling in your yard, wet spots that never dry, or lush patches of grass over your sewer line suggest a broken or blocked underground pipe—camera inspection is essential to plan repair.
  • Multiple slow drains throughout the home (sink, tub, toilet) all at once point to a main-line blockage or offset—requires main-line cleaning and possibly camera inspection to confirm the cause.

FAQ — Drains

What's the difference between cable and hydro-jetting?

Cable cleaning uses a motorized snake to break through blockages and works well for localized kitchen or bathroom clogs. Hydro-jetting uses high-pressure water to clear stubborn buildup and scour pipe walls—better for heavily calcified lines or main-line clogs. We recommend the right method based on what we find.

Do you always need to use a sewer camera for drain problems?

Not always. If it's a simple kitchen clog from grease or hair, cable cleaning often resolves it. But if the problem keeps happening, affects multiple fixtures, or we suspect root intrusion or a broken pipe, camera inspection gives us the full picture and helps prevent expensive repeat calls.

How can I prevent future drain backups?

Avoid pouring grease down kitchen drains—let it cool and throw it out. Use drain screens to catch hair in bathrooms. Don't flush paper towels, wipes, or feminine products down toilets—toilet paper only. We also recommend main-line cleaning every 5–7 years to prevent buildup, especially in older Phoenix homes with cast-iron pipe.
