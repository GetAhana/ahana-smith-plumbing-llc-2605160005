Plumbing repairs you can trust

Plumbing repairs are our bread and butter across Phoenix, Scottsdale, and surrounding neighborhoods. Whether it's a dripping faucet, running toilet, or a slow leak hidden behind a wall, our technicians diagnose the problem, show you your options, and fix it right the first time with upfront pricing and full cleanup.

Repair standards that hold up

How we handle Repairs

Our repair process is designed for clarity and confidence. From the moment you call until we leave your home, you stay informed and in control.

  • You call or text us — Describe the issue—a drip, a backup, a stain, or a spike in your bill. We listen, ask clarifying questions, and give you a realistic arrival window for diagnosis.
  • We diagnose and estimate — Our technician arrives with tools and experience, finds the source, and shows you the problem. We quote repair and replacement options, explain labor and material costs upfront, and answer your questions before you decide.
  • We repair and document — Once you approve, we complete the work, pressure-test the repair, clean up thoroughly, and provide photos and a detailed invoice. Every repair carries a one year labor warranty.
When to call us

Common signs you need plumbing repair service include visible water stains on walls or ceilings, the sound of running water when faucets are off, soft spots in flooring near plumbing fixtures, or a sudden spike in your water bill. If you notice any of these in your Phoenix home, call Smith Plumbing for a free diagnosis.

  • Water stains on walls, ceilings, or under sinks indicate an active leak needing immediate attention to prevent mold and structural damage.
  • Dripping faucets or running toilets waste water and money—a repair typically takes under an hour and costs far less than the wasted gallons over a year.
  • Unexplained jumps in your water bill often signal a hidden leak in supply lines, underground water mains, or slab foundations that should be located and repaired promptly.
  • Soft, spongy, or discolored flooring near plumbing fixtures suggests water damage from leaking pipes underneath—catch it early to avoid expensive subfloor replacement.
  • Low water pressure throughout your home or in one area can point to mineral buildup, a partially closed valve, or a hidden leak reducing flow to that zone.

FAQ — Repairs

How long does a typical plumbing repair take?

Most repairs take one to three hours depending on the issue. A dripping faucet may take 30 minutes, while a hidden leak behind a wall or a supply line repair might take longer. We give you a time estimate after diagnosis so you can plan your day.

Do you offer emergency repair service?

Yes. Burst pipes, water heater leaks, and major backups get priority dispatch. We respond the same day on weekdays and by the next day on weekends. Call or text immediately and we'll confirm your arrival window.

What warranty covers plumbing repairs?

All repair work, including new supply lines and fixture installations, carries a one year labor warranty. If the repair fails due to our workmanship within that year, we fix it at no charge. Materials carry manufacturer warranty, which we explain in your invoice.
